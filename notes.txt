  - Project structure: Create a separate Hetzner "Project" for this app — isolates billing and resources.
  - Firewall: Enable Hetzner's cloud firewall immediately after VM creation. Only open ports 22 (SSH), 80, 443.

  The Fuzzy Part: Secrets & Env Vars
                                                                                                                                                                          
  You don't need a UI. Here's how it works:
                                                                                                                                                                          
  Step 1 — SSH into your Hetzner VM and run:                                                                                                                              
  kubectl create secret generic app-secrets \
    --from-literal=MYSQL_PASSWORD=supersecret \                                                                                                                           
    --from-literal=MINIO_ACCESS_KEY=abc123 \   
    --from-literal=KEYCLOAK_ADMIN_PASSWORD=xyz                                                                                                                            
  This stores secrets encrypted inside k3s (never in git).
                                                                                                                                                                          
  Step 2 — Your Deployment manifest references them:                                                                                                                      
  envFrom:                                                                                                                                                                
    - secretRef:                                                                                                                                                          
        name: app-secrets
  k3s injects all secret keys as environment variables into the container — exactly like your current .env file, but managed by the cluster.
                                                                                                                                                                          
  To update a secret later: re-run kubectl create secret ... --dry-run -o yaml | kubectl apply -f -
